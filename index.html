Basmati Jollof Rice Recipe

Basmati Jollof Rice is a classic West African dish known for its vibrant color, rich flavor, and festive aroma. This recipe uses basmati rice for a fluffy, non-sticky texture. Perfect for family gatherings and celebrations!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 large tomatoes, blended
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 2 red bell peppers, blended
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers, blended (optional for heat)
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, etc.)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly in cold water until the water runs clear. Soak for 20 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add chopped onions and saut√© until translucent.
  3. Stir in the blended tomatoes, bell peppers, and scotch bonnet. Cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add tomato paste, thyme, curry powder, and bay leaves. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Pour in chicken stock and bring to a boil. Add the drained rice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  6. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot with foil and a lid. Let it cook for 20-25 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
  7. Stir in mixed vegetables, cover, and steam for another 5 minutes.
  8. Fluff the rice with a fork. Remove bay leaves and serve hot.
